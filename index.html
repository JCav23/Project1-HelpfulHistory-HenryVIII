Henry VIII

1491 - 1547

About Us


Helpful History is a series of educational websites dedicated to helping everybody learn about some of the most famous historical figures.

This first entry into our series examines the life of King Henry VIII, who sat on the English throne from 1509 until his death in 1547.

Here you will find information about details of some of the history he is known for, including his life at Hampton Court Palace, and the marriages to each of his six wives

You will also find a feedback page where you can let us know if we were helpful, share your ideas for any future pages, and vote on who should be the next historical figure in our series


Life at Hampton Court

Originally built in 1514 and located in the London Bourough of Richmond upon Thames, Hampton Court Palace became one of Henry VIII most favoured residences, and is now open to the public as a major tourist attraction. On this page you will find more information about King Henry's life and reign and more details about the palace.


Wives of Henry VIII

Henry VIII is best known for his six marriages, and his efforts to have his first marriage annuled which led to the English Reformation, the seperation of the Church of England from papal authority. On this page you will find more information about each of his spouses and how this brought about the changes in the Church.
